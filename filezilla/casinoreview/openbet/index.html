OpenBet in a Nutshell

History

OpenBet started life in 1996 in London, England under the name Orbis Technologies, a software development firm. Under the sharp leadership of David Loveday, the company expanded organically and through acquisitions. It's name was changed to OpenBet in 2010, to match its by then hugely popular and successful betting software platform of the same name. In April 2016, OpenBet was acquired by rival gaming software firm NYX Gaming for £270 million.

Games Overview

OpenBet has a tried and tested open gaming platform that allows its clients (casinos and other gaming sites) to offer their players OpenBet's portfolio of HD style games, alongside those of many other top-rated software vendors including IGT, Cryptologic and Wagerworks. Its games cover multiple categories like slots, roulette, blackjack, video poker, Caribbean stud poker and others. Its most popular slot titles include Pachinko, Sherlock's Reel Mystery, Lucky Star, Dead Mans Chest, Big Top Extravaganza, Pimp My Slot, Day at the Races, Chinese New Year and Battle of the Atlantic.

Software Features

OpenBet's online gaming software allows players to sign up for one account they can use for multiple platforms. This means you can use a single account to access and play in numerous currencies and languages at online, mobile and land gaming facilities. OpenBet's gaming development software of choice is HTML5 which lets you play games on your respective desktop computer browser or smart phone or tablet without downloads, installations or updates. In fact, OpenBet's game suite can be played on the majority of smart devices powered by Android, Windows or Apple iOS.

Software Accreditation

OpenBet's position as one of the UK, and indeed the world's, most respected online casino and betting software vendors can be gauged by its trophy cabinet. For instance, in 2015 at the eGaming Review B2b awards, OpenBet won Sports Betting Supplier of the Year for the sixth consecutive year. At that same ceremony, it picked up the In-Play Betting Software of the Year award, and in 2016 was honoured with the Sports betting Supplier of the Year award at the EGR B2B Australia Awards. OpenBet is licensed and regulated by the UK Gambling Commission as well as the Alderney Gambling Control Commission. It is also a member of the prestigious Remote Gambling Association (RGA).

Conclusion

Proof of OpenBet's success can be judged by the high calibre of online casinos and sportsbooks that use its software. Many are amongst the biggest and most successful gaming sites in the UK and beyond, which is why the huge acquisition price by NYX Gaming did not come as a surprise to many gaming industry insiders.
